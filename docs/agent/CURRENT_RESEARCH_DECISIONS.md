## Conditional-candidate rule

A failed unconditional contract may generate a new conditional hypothesis only when:

- the new claim is materially narrower;
- the context is causal and observable before entry;
- the original run decision remains unchanged;
- post-hoc origin is recorded;
- same-sample work is limited to explanation and controls;
- validation comes from independent or prospective evidence.

## Aggressive research interpretation

Aggressive means:

- preserve sparse and context-dependent opportunities;
- accept low capacity when suitable for the intended small account;
- permit convex payoff distributions when predeclared;
- pursue real controls for positive conditional objects;
- quantify risk rather than demand universal yearly uniformity.

Aggressive does not mean:

- relaxing authority or leakage controls;
- selecting years after outcomes;
- hiding concentration;
- using leverage to compensate for absent edge;
- combining weak correlated strategies into a false portfolio edge.

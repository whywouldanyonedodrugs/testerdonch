# Backtest Claims and Review Guide, rev5

Every result states:

```text
family and translation IDs
old lineage and redefinition reason
protocol phase
fold role and exposure class
search space and explored-cell count
candidate-selection method
payoff archetype
execution and cost contract
outer-evaluation status
multiplicity adjustment
route and limitation tags
validation and deployment status
```

Allowed:

> This redefined translation was selected in registered development folds and produced positive next-block evidence; the history is programme-exposed and remains unvalidated.

Not allowed:

> The old family was wrong, but the new backtest proves the family works.

Reviewers distinguish:

- translation failure;
- family exhaustion;
- measurement underidentification;
- insufficient search;
- development evidence;
- outer-fold evidence;
- prospective validation.

A result may be profitable yet narrow, convex, context-dependent or uncertain. State the claim scope rather than converting every limitation into a rejection.
